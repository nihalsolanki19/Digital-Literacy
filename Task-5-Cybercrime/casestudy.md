# Task 5 - Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

## Overview
[cite_start]This case study explores a common "Reverse QR Code" scam targeting college students selling items on online marketplaces[cite: 77, 78].

## The Scenario
1. [cite_start]**The Trap**: A student lists a used textbook or electronic gadget for sale on a platform like OLX[cite: 78, 79]. 
2. [cite_start]**The Contact**: A "buyer" contacts the student via WhatsApp, expressing urgent interest and offering to pay immediately via UPI[cite: 78].
3. [cite_start]**The Hook**: Instead of sending money, the scammer sends a QR code to the student, claiming, "Scan this to receive the payment into your bank account"[cite: 78].
4. **The Theft**: When the student scans the code and enters their UPI PIN, they are actually authorizing a *debit* from their account rather than a credit. [cite_start]The scammer instantly withdraws the funds[cite: 78].

## Target & Consequences
* [cite_start]**Target**: Young adults and students who are frequent users of digital payment apps but may not realize that a PIN is never required to *receive* money[cite: 78].
* [cite_start]**Consequences**: Immediate financial loss, potential compromise of linked bank account details, and significant emotional distress[cite: 78].
